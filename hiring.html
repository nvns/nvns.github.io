Job Summary


  Job Title: Program Director / Lead Teacher for Noe Valley Cooperative Nursery School
  Job Type: Part-time (30-33 hours per week when school is in session)
  Reports to: The Board of Directors of Noe Valley Nursery School
  Supervises: Paid Teacher (with Teacher permit)
  Job Starts: January 4, 2016

Job Description

Noe Valley Nursery School (NVNS) is seeking a new Director to lead our small preschool in a part-time, play-based program. Join our loving, learning community that is supportive of your growth and values your leadership. We are seeking someone with creativity and a passion for child-centered, play-based, and project-based education. A strong love for nature-based learning and community building is important.

The director will design curriculum and provide a healthy and stimulating environment for children to develop socially, physically, emotionally and intellectually. NVNS promotes child-centered activities, hands-on exploration of the physical environment and many opportunities to build social relationships with peers and adults. Children at NVNS learn how to pursue their own interests, discover new passions, respect others and have fun!

NVNS is a non-profit, non-sectarian, democratic organization where each family participates in the planning, fundraising, and operation of the school. Parents participate in the daily program by serving as assistants to the director. With strong leadership and communication skills, the director will be responsible for leading the community of families in upholding the vision and mission of the school.

NVNS is located in the Christopher Playground Clubhouse in San Francisco, adjacent to Glen Canyon and a community garden. This location provides our children with a range of indoor and outdoor activities. Every day, the director and 1 paid teacher work with 4-6 parents and up to 28 children to create an adult-to-child ratio of 1:5. NVNS has operated as a parent cooperative and maintained a commitment to play-based learning for over 45 years.

Responsibilities

  Pedagogy

  • Design all the curriculum integrating your own ideas and leveraging past curriculum.
  • Manage the daily activities of the school.
  • Incorporate elements of play-based education into all endeavors.
  • Assist children at their own developmental level to enhance social, emotional, and cognitive growth and adjust to the program and to their peers.
  • Respond appropriately to conflict with clear, consistent, and age-appropriate strategies.
  • Review and revise the program structure and curriculum using ideas from educational theory and input from other teachers and members.
  • Work closely with the paid teacher to guide instruction.

  Community Support and Communication

  • Support the growth and healthy development of the NVNS diverse parent community.
  • Support, supervise and mentor parent teachers during daily activities.
  • Develop an ongoing educational program to be implemented daily with parents and reinforced at the monthly parent education meetings.
  • Hold one-on-one parent-director conferences for families, as needed, with sensitivity towards confidentiality.
  • Work closely with the NVNS President to support the community and resolve any issues.
  • Engage in email communication in a timely manner.

  New Family Recruitment

  • Lead Parent Information meetings to recruit new members (1-3 nights yearly).
  • Conduct parent interviews and provide feedback to membership chairperson regarding the selection of new participating families each year.
  • Provide ongoing assessment of new families in the beginning of the year.

  Administrative and Licensing

  • Coordinate closely with the Board of Directors on all matters relevant to the operation of the school.
  • Implement and assure compliance with all licensing requirements.

  Professional Development & Support

  • Stay informed about current early childhood education research.
  • Choose professional development events to attend, funded by NVNS.
  • Meet with NVNS mental health consultant as needed.
  • Engage in open communication with the Board of Directors seeking additional support as needed.

  Time Commitment

  • Total Average Weekly Time commitment: 30-33 hours.
  • Salaried School Year runs from Aug 1st – May 31st. This includes planning, orientation, transition week, in-service days, and all regular school days. (Current start date: January 4, 2016)
  • On site time commitment: Monday – Friday: 8:30am – 1:30pm
  • Email correspondence and planning off site as needed.
  • In-person meetings (with paid teacher, board members, current or prospective parents) conducted as necessary for up to 30 min, between 1:00-1:30pm.
  • Group Meetings: Attendance is required at all information nights, as well as monthly board, business, and parent education meetings. Director is required to lead 7 of the parent education meetings and provide a short director’s report at the board and business meetings.
  • Summer: Director shall be available via email over the summer as needed.
  • Special Events: attend NVNS community events whenever possible.
  • Time Off: Up to 10 days paid sick leave and 5 days vacation throughout the salaried school year.
  • Holidays: NVNS follows the SFUSD calendar with the same school holidays, including Winter Break and Spring Break.
  • Optional: Afternoon program (with additional compensation), July summer program (with additional compensation).

Qualifications

  Required

  • Early Childhood Certification (Site Supervisor permit or equivalent) - http://ccld.ca.gov/res/pdf/ChildCareCtrProviderReqStaffQual.pdf
  • A minimum of 5 years prior experience working in early childhood education
  • Experience supervising a team and mentoring others
  • Strong knowledge of child development and a passion to grow in the field
  • Strong connection with child-centered, play-based philosophy, with an emphasis on inquiry-based learning
  • Ability to lead, relate to and empathize with children, parents, and co-workers
  • Ability to safely lift a minimum of 50 lbs and to bend, stoop, sit on the floor, climb stairs and walk

  Preferred

  • 10+ years experience working in early childhood education
  • Enjoys nature-based education
  • Experience building community
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills
  • Strong organizational skills and time management skills
  • Experience teaching English Language Learners and/or multiple language skills
  • Proficiency in a musical or artistic field

  Compensation: $45,000 yearly, $3,000 for retirement, $3,000 for health benefits.
